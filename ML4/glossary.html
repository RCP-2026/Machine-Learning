Glossary ML4
Dimensionality Reduction: The process of transforming data from a high-dimensional feature space into a lower-dimensional representation while preserving as much meaningful structure or variability as possible.
High-Dimensional Data: Data characterised by a large number of features or variables, often where many features are correlated or redundant.
Variance: A statistical measure of how widely data values are spread around their mean, used in PCA as a proxy for information content.
Projection: The operation of mapping data points onto a vector or subspace, typically by taking a dot product, to obtain a lower-dimensional representation.
Unit Vector: A vector with magnitude one, commonly used to define a direction for projection without scaling the data.
Principal Component Analysis (PCA): A linear dimensionality reduction technique that identifies orthogonal directions in feature space along which data variance is maximised.
Principal Component: An orthogonal direction in feature space that captures the maximum remaining variance after previous components have been accounted for.
Orthogonality: A geometric property indicating that two vectors are perpendicular, ensuring that principal components capture independent sources of variance.
Explained Variance: The amount of total data variance captured by a given principal component.
Explained Variance Ratio: The proportion of total variance accounted for by each principal component, often used to guide dimensionality selection.
Cumulative Explained Variance: The running total of explained variance across successive principal components, used to determine how many components are needed to retain sufficient information.
Feature Space: The coordinate system defined by the original variables or features of a dataset.
Component Space: The transformed coordinate system defined by the principal components after applying PCA.
Linear Transformation: A transformation that preserves linear relationships between variables, characteristic of PCA.
Feature Scaling: The process of standardising or normalising features prior to PCA to prevent variance being dominated by differences in scale.
Dimensionality Selection: The process of choosing how many principal components to retain based on explained variance or analytical goals.
Limitations of PCA: Constraints arising from PCA’s linearity and variance-based nature, including sensitivity to scaling and inability to capture non-linear structure.
